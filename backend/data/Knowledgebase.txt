## 3. Appointments & Registration

### Booking an Appointment
- **Online (Preferred):** Use the patient portal: [www.arogyamedcity.com/portal](http://www.arogyamedcity.com/portal)
- **Phone:** Call the main line (555) 100-2000 and select the "Appointments" option.
- **Walk-in:** Visit the registration desk in the main lobby. Walk-in appointments are subject to availability and wait times may be significant.

### Required Documents (New Patients)
- Government-issued photo ID (e.g., Aadhar Card, Passport, Driver's License)
- Valid insurance card
- Referral letter from a primary care physician (if required by your insurance plan)
- List of current medications

### Appointment Policies
- **Cancellation/Rescheduling:** Must be done at least 24 hours in advance via the patient portal or by calling the appointments desk.
- **Late Policy:** If you are more than 15 minutes late, your appointment may be rescheduled to avoid delaying other patients. Please call the department if you are running late.
- **No-Show Policy:** Patients who miss an appointment without providing 24-hour notice may be subject to a "no-show" fee.

### Wait-Time Expectations (Standard)
- **General OPD:** 15-30 minutes after scheduled time
- **Specialist Clinics:** 30-45 minutes after scheduled time
- **Emergency Room:** Varies based on triage priority. Critical cases are seen immediately, regardless of arrival time.

## 5. Visiting Policies & Hours

### General Visiting Hours
- **General Wards:** 4:00 PM - 7:00 PM daily
- **Intensive Care Unit (ICU):** 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM
- **Maternity Ward:** 11:00 AM - 1:00 PM and 5:00 PM - 7:00 PM
- **Pediatric Ward:**
  - One parent/guardian is allowed to stay 24/7
  - Other visitors: 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM

### General Visitor Rules
- **Limit:** Maximum two (2) visitors per patient at one time in the room
- **Child Visitors:** Children under 12 are generally not permitted to visit patient floors for infection control reasons. Exceptions may be made by the charge nurse.
- **Food:** Bringing outside food for patients is discouraged. Please check with the nurse first, as many patients are on restricted diets.
- **Noise:** Please be quiet and courteous in patient hallways.

### Infection Control & Safety
- **Masks:** Masks are mandatory in all patient care areas (rooms, hallways, waiting rooms)
- **Illness:** Visitors with symptoms of illness (fever, cough, cold) are not permitted to visit
- **Hand Hygiene:** All visitors must wash their hands or use hand sanitizer before and after visiting a patient's room

## 8. Emergency Triage Guidelines

### What to Expect in the ER
Patients are seen based on the severity of their condition, not the order of arrival. A triage nurse will assess you first to determine your priority level.

### Triage Levels
- **Level 1: Emergency (Go to ER Immediately)**
  - Chest pain or pressure
  - Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  - Severe bleeding
  - Sudden weakness, numbness, or drooping on one side of the body
  - Loss of consciousness
  - Severe head injury
  - Seizures
- **Level 2: Urgent (Contact doctor or visit urgent care/ER)**
  - High fever that doesn't respond to medication
  - Sprains or strains with severe pain or inability to walk
  - Minor cuts that are large or deep and may need stitches
  - Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
- **Level 3: Routine (Schedule a regular appointment)**
  - Cold or flu symptoms (unless severe)
  - Routine check-ups
  - Medication refills
  - Minor aches and pains

## 9. Common Reception FAQs (Quick Reference)

### General Questions
- **Q: Where is the nearest restroom?**
  - **A:** In the Main Lobby, down the hall past the coffee shop. Public restrooms are also near the main elevator on each floor.
- **Q: How do I get my lab reports?**
  - **A:** Lab reports are available on the patient portal within 48 hours. Physical copies can be collected from the Records Department in Wing D, Ground Floor.
- **Q: What are the cafeteria hours?**
  - **A:** Refer to Section 2 for cafeteria hours. The coffee shop is also open, and vending machines are available 24/7.
- **Q: How do I connect to the Wi-Fi?**
  - **A:** Connect to the Arogya-Guest network. No password is needed.
- **Q: Can I get a wheelchair for my relative?**
  - **A:** Yes, wheelchairs are available at the main entrance and the ER entrance. Ask a staff member or volunteer for assistance.
- **Q: I have a complaint about my care. Who do I talk to?**
  - **A:** Speak with the charge nurse on the floor, or contact Patient Relations at Ext. 8800. Their office is in Wing B on the ground floor.
- **Q: How do I find out a patient's room number?**
  - **A:** For privacy, the patient directory can be checked. Please spell the patient's full name. (Note: Only provide info if the patient has not opted out.)
- **Q: Where can I buy flowers or a gift?**
  - **A:** The gift shop is located in the main lobby, near the pharmacy.
